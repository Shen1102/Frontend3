About Information

Sabah, renowned for its stunning tropical islands and lush nature, is a premier destination for eco-adventures. The state's crowning jewel is Mount Kinabalu, one of Southeast Asia's highest peaks, while Sipadan is celebrated globally as a top diving destination. With a diverse population of around 3.1 million from 32 ethnic communities, Sabah offers a rich cultural experience alongside its natural wonders.

Kota Kinabalu, the capital formerly known as Jesselton, serves as the gateway to a variety of adventures such as diving, river cruising, mountain climbing, white-water rafting, and caving. Most of the city was rebuilt after World War II, with only three wartime buildings still standing. Discover Sabah, the 'Land Below the Wind,' for an unforgettable adventure.

Bohey Dulang Island

Bohey Dulang Island, the second largest in Sabah’s Tun Sakaran Marine Park, features a unique lagoon-like formation created by seawater flooding a volcanic crater. The surrounding sea is teeming with vibrant coral reefs, making it a haven for divers and snorkelers seeking stunning underwater views. Visitors can also hike to the island's highest peak at 353 metres for panoramic views and to explore the rare flora and fauna.

The island is a paradise for bird watchers, home to species such as hornbills, partridges, and black-naped fruit doves. Don’t miss the Giant Clam and Marine Invertebrate Hatchery to learn about rare giant clams and seaweed farming. Bohey Dulang is inhabited by the nomadic Bajau Laut, or Sea Gypsies, adding to the island’s unique allure. As there is no accommodation on the island, visitors can stay on nearby islands like Pom Pom and Mataking or in Semporna town, and join day trips to Bohey Dulang.

Sipadan Island

Situated about 20 miles from Semporna in Sabah, Sipadan is Malaysia’s sole oceanic island of volcanic origin, rising from a 600-metre abyss in the Celebes Sea. Originally a bird sanctuary declared in 1933, it became a marine reserve in 1981. Sipadan gained global recognition after French marine biologist Jacques Yves Cousteau featured its underwater marvels in his 1989 documentary, "Borneo: The Ghost of the Sea Turtle. The island is renowned for its exceptional diving, especially from February to November, when visibility ranges between 20 to 60 metres.

Known as the turtle capital of the world, Sipadan hosts large breeding colonies of green and hawksbill turtles and boasts over 3,000 species of fish and hundreds of coral species. Divers can explore 12 distinct dive sites around the island, including Coral Gardens, White-Tip Avenue, and Turtle Patch. As there is no accommodation on Sipadan, visitors typically stay on nearby islands like Mabul and Kapalai. Access to Sipadan requires a permit from Sabah Parks, with a daily limit of 120 permits issued to various resorts. Beyond Sipadan, the Celebes Sea offers additional stunning islands like Bum Bum and Sibuan.

Mantanani Island

Located in the northeast of Kota Belud, the Mantanani Islands—a trio of small islands including Mantanani Besar, Mantanani Kecil, and Lingisan—shine like gems off Sabah's coast. Among them, Mantanani Besar is the largest and only inhabited island. Its deep blue waters offer ideal conditions for swimming, snorkeling, diving, and fishing, teeming with diverse marine life such as schools of fish, blue-spotted rays, marble stingrays, seahorses, and even the rare dugong.

With its pristine white sandy beaches, Mantanani provides a tranquil escape for those seeking a secluded island retreat. Visitors can relax under the sun, enjoy the soothing sound of the waves, and savor the gentle breeze—making it an ideal destination for a romantic getaway.

Upside Down House of Borneo

The Upside Down House of Borneo, or Rumah Terbalik, offers a quirky and fun experience with its completely inverted furniture and décor. Visitors can capture amusing photos and enjoy a disorienting, yet memorable, experience as they explore this unique attraction with their families.

Gaya Street Sunday Market

The Gaya Street Sunday Market in Kota Kinabalu is a vibrant weekly event held every Sunday morning. This bustling marketplace, which closes off Gaya Street to traffic, is a must-visit for those wanting to dive into local culture, discover unique souvenirs, and savor delicious street food.

The market features a wide range of offerings, from fresh produce and local handicrafts to clothing, accessories, and plants. It’s a lively spot where visitors can enjoy the vibrant atmosphere and find treasures made by local artisans.

Sabah Museum

The Sabah Museum offers a comprehensive exploration of Sabah’s history and culture. With exhibits spanning archaeology, ethnography, and natural history, it provides insights into the region's earliest inhabitants, diverse cultures, and local flora and fauna. The museum also features a gallery showcasing art and artifacts from around the world, making it an ideal destination for introducing visitors of all ages to the rich heritage of Sabah.

Menara Tun Mustapha

Menara Tun Mustapha, also known as the Sabah Foundation Building, stands 122 meters tall as Sabah's tallest skyscraper and one of just four hanging structures worldwide with a glass facade. This iconic building features an Observation Deck offering panoramic views of Kota Kinabalu, a revolving restaurant for a unique dining experience, and the Sabah Foundation Museum, where visitors can delve into the region's history and culture.

Mount Kinabalu

At 4,095 meters (13,435 feet) above sea level, Mount Kinabalu is the highest peak in Southeast Asia, located in Sabah’s Ranau district within Kinabalu Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The mountain is renowned for its rich biodiversity, hosting 5,000 to 6,000 plant species, 326 bird species, and over 100 mammal species, making it a premier destination for nature lovers.

The climb to the summit typically takes two days and one night. Climbers stay at Panalaban (formerly Laban Rata) before continuing to Low’s Peak via the Ranau or Kota Belud trails. The descent can be challenging due to its steep and often slippery paths, especially after morning rains. With only 100 climbing permits issued daily, early booking is essential, and climbers must be accompanied by an accredited guide as per national park regulations.

Rainforest Discovery Centre

Sabah’s Rainforest Discovery Centre (RDC), situated in the Kabili-Sepilok Forest Reserve about 23 km from Sandakan, is a premier environmental education hub. It offers visitors an immersive experience in one of Sabah's most accessible rainforests while highlighting the importance of rainforest conservation. The centre features an exhibition hall, a Plant Discovery Garden, a small lake, and a network of marked jungle trails, allowing visitors to explore diverse forest paths and observe a range of wildlife, including civets, slow lorises, tarsiers, and orangutans.

The RDC is renowned for its impressive plant collection, including pitcher plants, 250 types of orchids, and the towering Sepilok Giant yellow seraya tree, estimated to be 800 to 1,000 years old. It is also a top bird-watching destination, hosting Sabah’s annual Borneo Bird Festival and attracting bird enthusiasts from around the globe.

Kinabalu Park

Malaysia’s first UNESCO World Heritage Site, Kinabalu Park, is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. Located on Sabah's west coast and established as a national park in 1964, the park spans 754 square kilometers and centers around the majestic Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia’s highest peak at 4,095.2 meters. Home to over 4,500 species of flora and fauna, including the world's largest pitcher plant, Nepenthes Rajah, Kinabalu Park offers a diverse array of nature-based activities.

Visitors can explore the park's eight jungle trails, with options ranging from the beginner-friendly 2.5-kilometer Mempening Trail to the challenging 5.6-kilometer Liwagu River Trail. For a more leisurely experience, the Botanical Garden provides a tranquil setting to learn about the park’s rare plant species. Additionally, the nearby Poring Hot Springs offer a relaxing soak, while attractions like the Butterfly Farm, Tropical Garden, and Poring Orchid Conservation Centre, which boasts around 1,200 orchid species, enhance the park's appeal. Don’t miss the Poring Canopy Walkway for a bird’s-eye view of the lush surroundings.

White Water Rafting Adventure

Kadamalan River in Tamparuli Town, Sabah, is a top destination for white-water rafting, featuring Class I-II rapids ideal for all ages and skill levels. Upon arrival, you'll receive a brief safety briefing from an experienced guide to ensure a secure experience. Then, you can dive into the excitement of rafting along the Kadamalan River.

Mari Mari Cultural Village

A visit to Sabah's Mari Mari Cultural Village offers an immersive experience into Borneo’s rich heritage and traditions. This cultural museum highlights the diverse ethnic groups of Sabah, including the Kadazan-Dusun, Rungus, Lundayeh, Bajau, and Murut, each with their own unique lifestyles and customs. Explore traditional houses, witness the making of blowpipes, and engage in activities like starting a fire with bamboo or trying out tattoo arts. The village also features cultural performances and allows visitors to sample traditional delicacies from each tribe. After the tour, a refreshing dip in Kiansom Waterfall provides a perfect ending to your visit.

Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah

Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah, Malaysia’s first floating mosque, sits elegantly above a lagoon, creating the illusion of being suspended over water. Built as a tribute by Sultan Mahmud to his mother, the mosque showcases a blend of Middle Eastern and Moorish architectural influences, highlighted by its white marble facade that gleams under the sunlight. Inside, the mosque features intricate bomanite paving and vibrant mosaic work throughout its passageways, courtyards, and ablution areas, enhancing its majestic appearance.

The mosque can accommodate up to 2,000 people, with separate prayer halls for men and women. Visitors can reach Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah by taking a bus from Terminal Bas Sepadu (TBS) to Kampung Panji, and then a taxi or e-hailing service to the mosque.

Masjid Kristal

Masjid Kristal, a striking mosque built from steel and glass, is a jewel on the river in Kuala Terengganu. Constructed between 2006 and 2008, this unique mosque is set on the water's edge, creating a captivating shimmer as it overlooks the river mouth. Its architecture blends Moorish and Gothic styles, featuring four minarets that gleam golden in the sunlight. Inside, the mosque is adorned with large crystal chandeliers, frosted glass windows, and intricate latticework, with a yellow mihrab showcasing elegant calligraphy.

Pu Toh Si Temple

Built in 1980, this Buddhist temple in Kota Kinabalu is a significant landmark dedicated to Guan Yin, the Chinese Goddess of Mercy, known for her compassion and kindness. Designed in traditional Chinese architectural style, the temple features authentic carvings, sculptures, and a vibrant roof. Visitors are greeted at the main entrance by a grand statue of Guan Yin.
