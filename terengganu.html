About Information

Hailed as the ‘Gem of the East Coast,’ Terengganu captivates with its rich culture and heritage. Its stunning coastline features aquamarine waters and powdery white sands, while a cluster of enchanting islands lies scattered across the South China Sea. Kuala Terengganu, the state's charming capital, serves as the gateway to this coastal paradise.

Beyond the shores, Terengganu offers breathtaking natural beauty with meandering rivers illuminated by fireflies, cascading waterfalls, and expansive lakes. The state is also renowned for its traditional crafts and picturesque coastal villages, inviting visitors to experience its unique Malay grace and splendor.

Pulau Perhentian

Pulau Perhentian, celebrated for its clear aquamarine waters, powdery beaches, and untouched tropical forests, is a top vacation destination. Ranked as the 13th best beach in the world by CNN in 2013 and the 5th best island to relax in a hammock by Lonely Planet in 2010, Perhentian lives up to its reputation with its serene and tranquil atmosphere. The island also serves as a vital water catchment area and remains a pristine jungle paradise.

The name Perhentian, meaning ‘a stopover,’ reflects its historical role as a haven for fishermen and traders on ancient routes between Siam and Terengganu. A 30-minute boat ride from Kuala Besut Jetty brings you to this idyllic spot. Perhentian offers diverse attractions and activities, from snorkeling and diving to relaxing on picturesque beaches. Pulau Perhentian Besar is ideal for families and couples seeking luxury and peace, while Pulau Perhentian Kecil features Long Beach and Coral Bay for lively and adventurous experiences. Turtle Beach on Perhentian Besar is a secluded spot known for sea turtles nesting during the season, and Romantic Beach offers an intimate setting for couples.

Pulau Kapas

Just a 20-minute boat ride from Merang Jetty, Pulau Kapas may be smaller than northern Terengganu’s Perhentian or Redang Islands, but it’s renowned for its stunning white sandy beaches and unique squid-breeding grounds. This island attracts attention during the squid jigging season from March to August. Known for its clear seawater, lush forests, and vibrant coral reefs, Pulau Kapas offers a serene escape with activities like snorkeling and kayaking. The name “Kapas,” meaning “cotton island,” perfectly reflects its pristine, cotton-like sands.

Pulau Redang

Pulau Redang, renowned for its pristine blue waters and white sandy beaches, is a popular destination for both travelers and locals. Accessible via a 45-minute speed boat ride from Merang Jetty or a 30-minute ride from Shahbandar Jetty, it offers excellent visibility from March to September, ideal for spotting fusiliers, turtles, humphead parrotfish, cuttlefish, and small black reef sharks. During the monsoon season, starting in October, many tourist facilities close and diving and boating activities are temporarily suspended. Highlights of Redang Island include snorkeling and diving amidst its vibrant marine life.

Terengganu Drawbridge

Opened in August 2019, the Terengganu Drawbridge is Southeast Asia’s first drawbridge, inspired by London’s iconic Tower Bridge. Spanning 638 meters across the Terengganu River, it connects Kuala Terengganu to Seberang Takir. The bridge rises 15 stories high with two towers linked by a sky bridge, featuring Islamic architectural motifs and green granite accents that echo the minarets of a mosque. At night, its vibrant lights and reflections on the river create a spectacular sight.

To visit the Terengganu Drawbridge, take bus number 102 to Hentian Bas Majlis Bandaraya Kuala Terengganu. From there, a taxi or e-hailing service will complete your journey to the bridge.

Pasar Payang

Pasar Payang, located in a bustling warehouse at the heart of Kuala Terengganu, is a vibrant market open daily from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. This market offers an array of local treasures, including traditional handicrafts, locally-made fish crackers, and beautiful batik prints, silk cloths, and songket. Visitors can enjoy the colorful display of fabrics and a wide selection of Terengganu’s specialty kuih and fresh vegetables as they explore the market's diverse stalls.

To reach Pasar Payang, take a bus from Terminal Bas Selatan (TBS) in Kuala Lumpur to Kuala Terengganu, followed by a 7-minute walk from the bus station. Alternatively, a taxi or e-hailing service will get you directly to the market in about 18 minutes from the Kuala Terengganu Bus Station.

Chinatown Terengganu

Chinatown in Terengganu is a hidden gem that offers a glimpse into the city's rich heritage. Stretching across old shophouses dating back to the late 1800s, this area preserves the precious Peranakan heritage and is believed to be one of the oldest Chinese settlements in Malaysia. Visitors can explore ancestral shophouses, clan townhouses, and temples, experiencing a unique blend of Chinese and Malay cultures. The streets are lined with colorful street art, historic buildings, and charming new cafes that maintain the heritage of the area.

To reach Chinatown, visitors can use Kuala Terengganu's Bas Bandar service, which operates uniquely designed buses resembling traditional Malay houses. For a flat fee of RM1, passengers can travel from any station along the route and alight at Hentian Bas Majlis Bandaraya Kuala Terengganu, just an 8-minute walk from Chinatown.

Terengganu State Museum

The Terengganu State Museum, sprawling across 27 hectares with four interconnected buildings, showcases Terengganu’s architectural elegance with its concrete stilts, landscaped gardens, and intricate roofs. Inspired by local palatial designs, the museum houses nine diverse galleries, including those dedicated to natural history, textiles, Islamic art, and maritime history. Visitors can explore old maritime vessels, reconstructed traditional Malay houses, and the Batu Bersurat with its classical Malay inscriptions, alongside a vast collection of textiles, historical artifacts, and royal regalia.

To visit the museum, take a bus from Terminal Bersepadu Selatan (TBS) to Kampung Panji, and then a short seven-minute taxi or e-hailing ride will take you directly to the Terengganu State Museum.

Chemerong Forest Reserve

Experienced trekkers and nature enthusiasts should explore the Chemerong-Berembun-Langsir (CBL) track in Chemerong Forest Reserve, Hulu Dungun, Terengganu. This challenging 27-kilometre trail leads to the stunning Chemerong Waterfall, cascading 370 metres high, rewarding hikers with breathtaking views. The hike typically spans three days and two nights, featuring activities like climbing a 200-metre mountain, swimming in Jeram Lesung Pool, and marveling at giant Chengal trees and hollow trunks.

The CBL trail is demanding and suited for hikers with at least 2,000 metres of prior experience. The ideal hiking season is from May to July, as the trail is closed during the monsoon season from late October to mid-March due to heavy rainfall.

Pulau Tenggol

Pulau Tenggol, or Tenggol Island, is one of Malaysia's most unique destinations, situated in Dungun, Terengganu. This island, which was only recently "discovered" and previously uninhabited, has quickly become a favored tourist spot. In recent years, Tenggol Island has been developed with accommodations and facilities to cater to visitors, transforming it into a sought-after getaway while maintaining its distinctive charm and natural beauty.

Lata Payung Blue Lagoon

Lata Payung Waterfall, nestled in the Lata Payung Recreational Forest on the border of Setiu and Besut districts, is about 25 km from Bandar Seri Langkap and 65 km from Kuala Terengganu City. Accessible by car, bus, or taxi from Seri Langkap, visitors must follow a designated path to reach the waterfall's summit. Though less known compared to Sekayu and Chemerong Waterfalls, Lata Payung is renowned for its beauty and tranquil setting, offering a more secluded experience.

Kenyir Lake

Kenyir Lake, Malaysia's largest man-made lake and one of the biggest in Southeast Asia, is situated in Terengganu. Established in 1985 with the construction of the Kenyir Dam, the lake was originally created for hydroelectric power generation. In addition to its primary function, the dam also plays a crucial role in flood control and providing water supply to the surrounding region.

Masjid 8 Kubah

Built in 2012, Masjid 8 Kubah stands out with its eight vibrant domes reminiscent of Moscow's St. Basil's Cathedral. This eye-catching mosque, set amidst a scenic paddy field, replaced an old wooden mosque that once accommodated over 500 people. To visit, take a bus from Terminal Bas Selatan (TBS) in Kuala Lumpur to Jerteh and then a 12-minute taxi or e-hailing ride to the mosque. Alternatively, you can travel from TBS to Pengkalan Kubor in Kuala Terengganu and take a 19-minute taxi or e-hailing service to the mosque.

Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah

Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah, Malaysia’s first floating mosque, sits elegantly above a lagoon, creating the illusion of being suspended over water. Built as a tribute by Sultan Mahmud to his mother, the mosque showcases a blend of Middle Eastern and Moorish architectural influences, highlighted by its white marble facade that gleams under the sunlight. Inside, the mosque features intricate bomanite paving and vibrant mosaic work throughout its passageways, courtyards, and ablution areas, enhancing its majestic appearance.

The mosque can accommodate up to 2,000 people, with separate prayer halls for men and women. Visitors can reach Masjid Tengku Tengah Zaharah by taking a bus from Terminal Bas Sepadu (TBS) to Kampung Panji, and then a taxi or e-hailing service to the mosque.

Masjid Kristal

Masjid Kristal, a striking mosque built from steel and glass, is a jewel on the river in Kuala Terengganu. Constructed between 2006 and 2008, this unique mosque is set on the water's edge, creating a captivating shimmer as it overlooks the river mouth. Its architecture blends Moorish and Gothic styles, featuring four minarets that gleam golden in the sunlight. Inside, the mosque is adorned with large crystal chandeliers, frosted glass windows, and intricate latticework, with a yellow mihrab showcasing elegant calligraphy.

Terengganu Heritage House

Terengganu, once part of the Malay kingdom of Langkasuka from the 2nd to 16th century, had a vibrant trading relationship with Siam, Cambodia, and Indonesia, which influenced its traditional architecture. Terengganu houses, built from cengal wood and roofed with "Singhorra Tiles" from Songkla, feature distinctive peles (gable ends) and intricate "Sobek" woodcarvings showcasing flora and Islamic motifs. Elevated about 2.5 meters off the ground, these homes create the appearance of animals with many legs and are designed to prevent flooding during monsoon rains, as they are often situated near river bends.
