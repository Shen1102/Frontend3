About Information

Johor Baru, the capital city of Johor state and Malaysia’s second-largest city, is always bustling with activity. Located at the border with Singapore, this once sleepy fishing town has transformed into a popular tourist destination known for its unique architecture and delicious cuisine.

The city enjoys tropical weather year-round, making it an ideal travel spot during both peak (June to August) and off-peak seasons (November to April). Just outside Johor Baru, you can immerse yourself in nature at Desaru, where you’ll find great trekking opportunities and miles of beaches perfect for relaxation.

Rawa Island.

Located 16 km off the coast of Mersing, Johor, Rawa Island offers a serene escape with its white sandy beaches, clear waters, and stunning views. Owned by the Sultan of Johor, this small island is surrounded by vibrant coral reefs and features a range of exciting activities, including water sports and jungle trekking in its lush tropical forest.

Visitors can enjoy refreshing swims in the clean waters and try the popular Rawa Slide for a fun dive into the sea. Diving and snorkeling are popular due to the island's rich marine life and colorful coral reefs. Additionally, island hopping tours to nearby Harimau and Mensirip islands offer unique food experiences. Nature enthusiasts can hike to the island's hilltop for panoramic views of the South China Sea and a chance to explore local flora and fauna. The trek is short, taking less than 30 minutes, with clear signages guiding the way.

Pulau Harimau

Don’t be misled by its name—Pulau Harimau has no tigers! The island’s name, meaning "Tiger Island," comes from its tiger-like shape seen from above.

Pulau Harimau offers pristine beauty with its clear blue waters and vibrant coral reefs. Though overnight stays are not allowed, visitors can explore Gua Harimau, a sea cave that’s accessible for swimming. For the daring, climbing the cave’s sides to dive from a high ledge into the water below adds an exciting splash to the visit.

Pulau Hujung

Previously known as Pulau Babi Hujung on some maps, Pulau Hujung is an uninhabited island offering a perfect escape from city life and human activity. Its remote setting and minimal signs of civilization make it an ideal destination for those seeking solitude.

The island is surrounded by vibrant coral reefs, visible with just a short swim from the shore. Equipped with a good pair of goggles, you'll discover a rich underwater world teeming with marine life. However, be cautious of sea urchins, which can be painful if stepped on. Visitors from nearby resorts often come here for picnics, so remember to bring your own food, as there are no facilities available on the island.

Tan Hiok Nee Heritage Street

The Old Town in JB is a vibrant area filled with colorful murals and historic buildings, perfect for a day of exploration and photography. Among its highlights is Tan Hiok Nee Heritage Street, named after one of the city's founding figures. This historic street is lined with antique shops, boutique cafes, and restaurants, offering a delightful escape from the heat and a chance to sample local flavors.

Johor Premium Outlets

Indulge in a shopping spree at JPO, a paradise for shopaholics where luxury brands are available at discounted prices. With over 150 outlets, including Coach, Bally, DKNY, Fossil, Michael Kors, Nike, Adidas, Armani Exchange, and Timberland, you'll find plenty to explore. When hunger strikes, the mall offers a variety of dining options, including a food court, cafes, dessert shops, and restaurants.

Pasar Karat

Pasar Karat is a vibrant flea market renowned for its wide array of secondhand treasures, including clothes, antiques, and unique knick-knacks. Opening only in the evenings, it comes alive after 8 pm with a bustling atmosphere. After dinner, take a casual stroll through the market, where you might enjoy live performances from talented street buskers.

Menara Jland

Visit JB city's inaugural aerial entertainment space, which debuted in 2019. Located 149 meters above ground in Menara Jland, it features a curved observation deck with glass flooring, offering breathtaking panoramic views of Johor and Singapore. In addition to the stunning vistas, the space includes a variety of VR and interactive games, along with photo spots perfect for capturing Instagram-worthy shots.

Legoland Malaysia

Legoland Malaysia is the country's first international theme park and a must-visit for Lego enthusiasts. Whether you grew up building with Lego bricks or enjoyed the Lego movie, you'll find plenty to do here. The park features exciting roller coasters, a driving school, a water park, and the Sea Life Aquarium zone. Plan to spend a day or two exploring all the attractions and fun experiences.

Angry Birds Activity Park

The Angry Birds Activity Park is packed with obstacle courses, a go-kart track, giant foam pits, and a marksmanship gallery, ensuring that kids have a blast and leave their frustrations behind. While the children enjoy endless fun, parents can relax in the Parent’s Lounge or grab a bite at the Snack Bar Café.

Johor Zoo

As one of Southeast Asia’s earliest zoos, Johor Zoo offers a budget-friendly experience with over a hundred animal species. Spend an afternoon spotting hippos, tigers, camels, and the elusive Malayan Sun Bear.

Taman Merdeka

Enjoy some fresh air at Taman Merdeka, Johor Bahru's largest public park. Ideal for any time of day, this expansive green space features an open-air theatre, a monument commemorating Malaysia's independence, rock sculptures, a clock tower, and more. It's a perfect spot for a relaxing evening, a jog, or a picnic with loved ones.

Arts & Culture in Muar, Royal Town

Muar, one of Johor's largest cities, blends vibrant heritage with modern charm. Known for its rich history and clean streets, Muar offers an array of attractions including local culinary delights like mi bandung and mi siput. The city is celebrated for its furniture industry, ranking as Malaysia's top exporter of rubberwood furniture. Additionally, Muar stands out as the birthplace of traditional dances such as ghazal and kuda kepang, and was recognized for its innovative use of augmented reality murals.

Visitors can explore Muar's cultural depth through sites like Muar Leather Craft, where you can see high-quality leather goods and participate in DIY workshops. The Songkok Masood Hussin boutique showcases traditional Malay headgear, attracting both dignitaries and international customers. For art enthusiasts, the Muar Art Gallery offers insights into local rattan furniture craftsmanship and woodworking, while Muar’s street art and Keraton Embah Anang provide a glimpse into the city’s artistic and historical heritage.

Johor Bahru Old Chinese Temple

Dating back to the 19th century, the Johor Bahru Old Chinese Temple is a significant cultural and religious site dedicated to five revered Chinese deities. Locally known as the Old Temple, it stands out with its vibrant red and white hues amid the surrounding modern grey skyscrapers, making it a must-visit for those interested in cultural landmarks and religious history.

Istana Besar

Built in 1866, the grand Istana Besar was once the residence of the Sultan of Johor. This majestic palace features a stunning fusion of Malay and Western architectural styles, highlighted by its striking white walls and distinctive blue roof. Currently, it serves primarily as a venue for official functions, with part of the palace transformed into the Royal Abu Bakar Museum, which displays a rich collection of antiques and royal heirlooms.

Medan Selera Meldrum Walk

To truly experience a city, dive into its local street food scene! Medan Selera Meldrum Walk is a vibrant night market where food lovers can savor local delights. From BBQ chicken wings and fried kway teow to satay and fresh seafood, there's something for everyone. Don’t miss the oyster omelette, a standout dish that attracts crowds and keeps them coming back for more well into the night!
