About Information

Penang is an amazing holiday destination and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It’s known for its stunning natural beauty and many cultural attractions, drawing in visitors from around the world. In its capital, George Town, you can explore a variety of interesting historical landmarks. Penang beautifully blends local culture with international influences, making it a great choice whether you're visiting for the first time or coming back for another visit.

The state of Penang offers endless tourist attractions that will enrich and satisfy you throughout your trip. The activities are perfect for both adults and children, making it an ideal destination for families or groups.

Batu Ferringhi Beach

As Penang's second most popular destination (after George Town), its long white sandy beach is truly a sight to see. You can bask in the sun or relax on the beach in peace, or get active with canoeing or windsurfing. For a thrilling experience, try parasailing and enjoy stunning views of the beach from above. The night market here is quite famous, and the waters are popular for a range of water sports like windsurfing, jet-skiing, and parasailing. After your adventures, take a stroll along Jalan Batu Ferringhi, where you'll find a variety of dining options.

Monkey Beach

Teluk Duyung, also known as Monkey Beach, is located in Penang National Park. The beach is named after the Crab-eating Macaques that live in the area. It’s one of the most stunning beaches on Penang Island, featuring a long stretch of white sand and clear waters ideal for snorkeling and swimming. Monkey Beach is a popular spot on weekends and public holidays, when many locals come to enjoy picnics with friends and family. If you like camping, there are campsites nearby where you can spend the night under the stars!

Pulau Jerejak

Pulau Jerejak is a serene tropical island located just off the southeast coast of Penang. It boasts picturesque beaches and a pristine rainforest that has been preserved in its original state for over 400 years. The 362-hectare island has a rich history, serving as the landing point for Penang's founder, Francis Light, in early 1786 and as Malaysia’s version of Alcatraz from 1969 to 1993.

Today, Pulau Jerejak is Penang’s main ecotourism destination, featuring a rainforest resort, an on-site spa, a restaurant, and various recreational facilities. It's a fantastic spot for activities such as kayaking, archery, paintball, jungle trekking, water polo, and beach volleyball. To reach the island, you can take a daily ferry from Penang Island’s Jerejak Jetty to the Jerejak Resort Jetty.

Georgetown

George Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, beautifully combines the vibrant traditions of its multicultural population into a captivating tapestry of sights, sounds, and smells. From colorful murals and historic pre-war buildings to a wide range of innovative museums and delicious local dishes that will leave you craving more, George Town is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Penang.

Komtar’s The Top

TOP Penang is a world-class tourist destination that deserves recognition for its outstanding contribution to the state’s tourism industry. It features the World’s First Tower Curved Skywalk, situated 251 meters above sea level. TOP Penang also boasts the largest indoor theme park and is known as the highest iconic tower in Penang. Visitors can enjoy 360-degree panoramic views of George Town from the 68th floor. Additionally, TOP Penang includes several other attractions: the Jurassic Research Center, Rainbow Skywalk, GP META Kart, Tech Dome Penang, Penang State Gallery, and the TOP Boutique Aquarium.

Penang House of Music

Penang's dedication to showcasing its rich cultural heritage is exemplified by the Penang House of Music. This unique venue offers an immersive experience into the island's musical traditions, featuring a resource center where enthusiasts can delve into Penang's melodic history. True to its name, the museum allows visitors to explore a diverse range of global music, including Indian folk and Chinese opera, making it a captivating destination for music lovers.

Penang Bridge

The Penang Bridge, often compared to London Bridge, is a vital 13.5 km-long suspension bridge linking Penang Island with the mainland. Built to alleviate the increasing ferry traffic and accommodate the growing population, it stands as Malaysia's second-longest bridge and the fifth-longest in South Asia.

Busy with commuters and event-goers alike, the Penang Bridge is a popular venue for major marathons. A photo from this iconic bridge will undoubtedly garner plenty of admiration and appreciation.

Penang Butterfly Farm

Anyone who loves flora and fauna should visit the Entopia Butterfly Farm in Batu Ferringhi. Here, you’ll find over four thousand stunning butterflies from more than a hundred different species, all fluttering around in a large, colorful garden. Some of the rare species include the Indian Leaf butterfly, Yellow Bird butterfly, and Wing butterfly. The farm also serves as a conservation center dedicated to caring for and breeding the habitats of these endangered species.

Penang National Park

Penang National Park is the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, offering a chance to connect with nature. The park features several trails leading to beautiful small beaches and an ancient lighthouse dating back to 1883. Some paths will take you to Kerachut Beach, home to a Turtle Conservation Centre, which is also an ideal spot for a picnic and a day out in the park.

Dove Jetty

Just like the setting of a mystery novel, the worn footpath and weathered shack at Dove Jetty holds its own history and charm. Jelutong Fisherman’s Wharf, or Dove Jetty as it is popularly known, is a makeshift pier erected by fisherfolk to moor their boats and keep their nets, tanks and other fishing equipment. The jetty is believed to have been named after the Harmony Fly seagull sculpture along Tun Dr. Lim Chong Eu Expressway. Although the simple wooden structure is not the main tourist attraction, but it remains as a nostalgic landmark of the days gone by and serves as a tribute to the lives of the state’s fisherfolk. A casual stroll here will give a chance to catch impressive views of the sunrise. It also makes an intriguing backdrop for your social media posts!

Escape Theme Park

Penang's first theme park offers an exciting mix of thrilling rides and water attractions. Nestled in a scenic natural setting, it enhances the adventure experience, particularly with activities like bungee jumping.

In addition to traditional lazy pools and water slides, the park features unique adventures such as the Flying Fox, Go Ape, Gecko Tower, and Monkey Business. These attractions include rock climbing, traversing suspension bridges, and uphill climbs on a seat, promising a diverse and exhilarating experience for visitors.

Pinang Peranakan Mansion

Located on Church Street in George Town, Penang, the Pinang Peranakan Mansion is a museum that highlights the rich cultural heritage of the Peranakan community. Built by prominent businessman Chung Keng Kwee in the late 19th century, the mansion originally served as his home and office. It showcases the fusion of Chinese and local Malay cultures, featuring an impressive collection of Peranakan antiques and artifacts that reflect the opulent lifestyle of the Babas and Nyonyas.

The museum’s interior is divided into distinct sections, each offering a glimpse into Peranakan life. Highlights include intricately decorated dining rooms with Chinese and European influences, a Bridal Chamber showcasing traditional artwork, and the Nyonya Kitchen with rare cooking utensils. Visitors can also enjoy local dishes at a small café on-site. The mansion’s exterior is adorned with Chinese figure paintings, an ancestral altar, and a water fountain, adding to its historical charm.

Kek Lok Si Temple

As the largest Buddhist temple in Malaysia, Kek Lok Si features a vast complex divided into three main areas. The temple includes a turtle liberation pond, giant statues of the Goddess of Mercy Guan Yin, a series of monasteries, and beautifully landscaped gardens. Known as the "Temple of Supreme Bliss," Kek Lok Si has become a national icon and is famous throughout Malaysia. It also houses historic relics, including imperial Buddhist sutras presented by the Manchu Emperor Guangxu and Empress Cixi during the Qing Dynasty.

Penang Street Art

Visitors can explore the history of George Town and its diverse multicultural residents through murals scattered around the city, as well as over 50 wrought-iron caricatures that humorously depict the lifestyle of early 20th-century immigrants. To enhance the experience, visitors can use a street art map to navigate and discover all the mural paintings more efficiently.

Penang Snake Temple

As its name suggests, the Penang Snake Temple is home to numerous snakes, which began to inhabit the temple after it was built in honor of Chor Soo Kong, a revered healer. The temple, also known as The Temple of Azure Cloud, is a unique attraction where visitors can observe snakes living comfortably in their natural habitat. This intriguing site is one of the must-visit places in Penang for those interested in seeing these reptiles up close.
