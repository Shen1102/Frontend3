About Information

Putrajaya, Malaysia’s ‘Intelligent Garden City’, is a modern administrative capital renowned for its striking architecture, expansive parks, and a scenic man-made lake. Central to the city is Putra Square, surrounded by uniquely designed buildings and bridges. The iconic pink-domed Putra Mosque stands out as a key landmark. Easily accessible from Kuala Lumpur, Putrajaya offers a blend of natural beauty and innovative urban planning, making it an ideal destination for your next holiday.

Floria Beach

Floria Beach, a manmade attraction in Anjung Floria, opened on March 1, 2022. Spanning 100 meters, it features a water fountain and ample seating. Visitors can enjoy activities such as fishing, kayaking, and picnicking by Anjung Floria lake. The beach offers convenient facilities including a cyclist café, surau, public toilets, and spacious parking. Open daily from 9 am to 6:30 pm, it’s an accessible and versatile spot for relaxation and recreation.

Pulau Papan

Pulau Papan is a serene island known for its stunning landscapes and historic colonial lighthouse. Featuring a mini agriculture park with diverse Bornean plant life, it’s a popular weekend retreat for locals and Bruneians seeking relaxation. Activities include snorkeling, jungle trekking, picnicking, beach volleyball, and barbecuing. While the island offers many enjoyable experiences, note that swimming and water activities are at your own risk due to the absence of lifeguards.

Pohon Batu Beach

Labuan is renowned for its stunning sunsets and picturesque beaches, making it a must-see for tourists. Pohon Batu Beach, with its wooden stalls selling fresh seafood, is a great spot to unwind and enjoy the view. Nearby, a sandy park features a Challenge Corner, perfect for team-building activities. After soaking up the beauty of Labuan, don’t miss the chance to shop for duty-free items like perfumes, alcohol, and chocolates. The island also offers excellent accommodation options, including the popular Grand Dorsett Labuan Hotel, which is conveniently located near the town.

Seri Wawasan Bridge

The Seri Wawasan Bridge, also known as Bridge No. 9, is a standout feature of Putrajaya, renowned for its futuristic design resembling a sailing ship. It’s especially stunning at night with its vibrant light display. Visible from afar, the bridge offers spectacular sunset and nighttime views, making it perfect for photography with the lake and city skyline as a backdrop.

Alamanda Putrajaya

Alamanda Shopping Centre, located in Precinct 1, is the largest mall in Putrajaya, featuring over 160 brand stores. It offers diverse dining options, including bakeries and international cuisine, and boasts the Esplanade dining area with a light show. Attractions also include the Superstar Karaoke Box and a bowling center.

IOI City Mall Putrajaya

IOI City Mall, located in IOI Resort City, Putrajaya, is the largest mall in South Klang Valley. Spanning four stories, it features over 350 outlets, including diverse dining options like Ramen Village and Starbucks, along with a theme park and an Olympic-sized skating rink. The Symphony Walk, with its dazzling fountains, offers a romantic setting for dining.

Palace of Justice

The Palace of Justice (Istana Kehakiman) in Putrajaya, inaugurated in 2004, is a striking example of Islamic, Moorish, Palladian, and Neoclassical architecture. Surrounded by lush gardens, it features grand domes and minarets. The five-story building houses federal and appeal courts, a library, and a museum showcasing Malaysia's judicial system. Visitors can explore its vibrant interiors, enjoy national events like the Colours of Malaysia fest, and gain insights into the country’s legal framework.

Putrajaya Botanical Garden

Putrajaya Botanical Garden, Malaysia’s largest botanical garden, is located in Precinct 1. Known locally as Taman Botani, it’s a prime spot for nature lovers, cyclists, and picnickers. The garden is divided into three sections: flowers, ornamental plants, and the preservation/research center. Key attractions include the Explorer’s Trail, Floral Gardens, Sun Garden, Palm Hill, and Lakeside. Visitors can enjoy a seafood restaurant, gift shop, and rentals for bicycles and paddle boats.

Putrajaya Lake

Putrajaya Lake, one of Malaysia's largest man-made lakes, spans 650 hectares in the heart of the city. It serves as a natural cooling system and enhances connectivity by linking Putrajaya with neighboring cities. The lake, which hosts landmarks like the Putra Mosque and Millennium Monument, is a hub for international water sports, having hosted events such as the F1 Powerboat World Championship, the Asian Canoeing Championship, and the Red Bull Air Races. Its scenic beauty and strategic importance make it a central feature of Putrajaya.

Putrajaya Wetlands Park

The Putrajaya Wetlands Park, or Taman Wetlands, is Malaysia’s largest artificial freshwater wetland, spanning 138 hectares. It features diverse flora and fauna, including over 100 bird species, making it a top birdwatching destination. The park’s lush greenery acts as the city's "green lungs," and its nature trails and botanical gardens provide a serene escape from urban life.

Putrajaya Equestrian Park

Putrajaya Equestrian Park, a 70-acre facility in Precinct 5, features a polo field, grandstand, paddock, and dressage arena. It hosts international and national equestrian events, including the FEI World Cup Qualifier. The park offers a public riding school with coaching packages for all ages and levels, fun pony rides, and personal photoshoots with the horses.

Putra Mosque

The Putra Mosque, or "Pink Mosque," is a prominent landmark in Putrajaya, known for its rose-tinted Arabic-Islamic architecture. Overlooking Putrajaya Lake, it offers guided tours for non-Muslim visitors, complete with informational leaflets in multiple languages. The mosque features funeral services, a dining hall, an auditorium, lecture rooms, and a learning center.

Moroccan Pavilion Putrajaya

The Moroccan Pavilion Putrajaya, or Astaka Morocco, showcases stunning Moorish architecture and symbolizes the strong bond between Malaysia and Morocco. Spanning 18,000 square feet, it features intricate carvings, colorful mosaics, and Arabic calligraphy, reflecting Morocco's imperial heritage. Completed over two years with authentic Moroccan materials and artisans, the pavilion includes four unique galleries, a cedarwood scent, and beautiful pools and fountains. Entrance is RM3 for adults and RM1 for students, with tickets available at the nearby Putrajaya Botanical Gardens Visitor Centre.

Tuanku Mizan Zainal Abidin Mosque

The Tuanku Mizan Zainal Abidin Mosque, or Iron Mosque, is the largest mosque in Putrajaya and stands gracefully by Poudre Lake. Renowned for its modern design and use of advanced technology, it offers a stunning view both day and night. The mosque's striking appearance is enhanced by its illumination after dark, making it a must-visit attraction for both tourists and pilgrims.

Perdana Putra

Perdana Putra, perched atop a hill in Putrajaya, serves as the Prime Minister’s office and a symbol of Malaysia's prosperity and heritage. The complex includes various government sections, such as the Prime Minister's and Deputy Prime Minister's offices, meeting halls, and VIP rooms. Its grand exterior and opulent interior are complemented by guided tours and interactive sessions that offer insights into the government’s operations and ideals.
