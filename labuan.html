Labuan, situated to the north of Brunei Bay and facing the South China Sea, is Malaysia’s only deep-water anchorage and a major international business and financial center. Covering 92 sq km, Labuan comprises the main island and six surrounding islets. Known as 'The Pearl of Borneo,' Labuan offers a unique blend of business and leisure. Visitors can explore dive sites close to the shore, World War II relics, tax-free shopping, and an international sea sports complex, making it a vibrant destination with a wide range of attractions.

Pantai Layang-Layangan

Located on Labuan's west coast, Pantai Layang Layangan, also known as United Nations Beach or UN'08, earned its title for being the cleanest beach in 2008. This pristine 6km stretch, stretching from Layang Layangan to Surrender Point, is perfect for sunset views and nature enthusiasts, with its sandy shores showcasing natural art from sand crabs and other critters.

Facing the South China Sea, the beach offers a serene setting for evening strolls and relaxation. As night falls, the area comes alive with vibrant parties, live music, and delicious food from nearby restaurants.

Pulau Papan

Pulau Papan is a serene island known for its stunning landscapes and historic colonial lighthouse. Featuring a mini agriculture park with diverse Bornean plant life, it’s a popular weekend retreat for locals and Bruneians seeking relaxation. Activities include snorkeling, jungle trekking, picnicking, beach volleyball, and barbecuing. While the island offers many enjoyable experiences, note that swimming and water activities are at your own risk due to the absence of lifeguards.

Pohon Batu Beach

Labuan is renowned for its stunning sunsets and picturesque beaches, making it a must-see for tourists. Pohon Batu Beach, with its wooden stalls selling fresh seafood, is a great spot to unwind and enjoy the view. Nearby, a sandy park features a Challenge Corner, perfect for team-building activities. After soaking up the beauty of Labuan, don’t miss the chance to shop for duty-free items like perfumes, alcohol, and chocolates. The island also offers excellent accommodation options, including the popular Grand Dorsett Labuan Hotel, which is conveniently located near the town.

Labuan International Sea Sport Complex

The Labuan International Sea Sport Complex, spanning 5.91 acres, is a premier water sports center and a highlight of Labuan's waterfront. Opened in 2001, it hosts notable events such as the Labuan International Sea Challenge, Rolex IGFA International Game Fishing Tournament, and Round Island Kayak Challenge. The complex features a unique architectural design, including an administrative block, a marine biology museum, eateries, and souvenir shops. Visitors can enjoy local food, shop for duty-free items like perfumes, alcohol, and chocolates, and explore Labuan’s diverse accommodations. The Grand Dorsett Labuan Hotel, the island's only five-star hotel, offers spacious rooms, a pool, and a gym, making it a popular choice for tourists.

The Chimney

Built in the 1980s, the Labuan Chimney remains a mystery, with locals speculating it could be a ventilation shaft, an unfinished mansion, or even a lighthouse. Standing 106 feet tall and constructed from 23,000 imported red bricks, the chimney features British-style architecture. Nearby, the Chimney Museum offers insights into its construction with brick samples and reinforcement materials. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as Labuan Bird Park and Taman Damai. For accommodation, the Manikar Beach Resort provides luxurious rooms, a swimming pool, and beach volleyball. Labuan’s duty-free shops offer a variety of discounted souvenirs, including wines, perfumes, and chocolates.

Labuan Square

Labuan Square is a key venue for major events, including Malaysia Day and Labuan's Independence Day celebrations. The park features a stunning landscape with lush lawns, vibrant flowers, and water fountains. Visitors enjoy strolling and taking photos against this picturesque backdrop. At the center is an elevated stage adorned with Malaysian flags, while a replica of the mysterious chimney is nearby. The square also showcases colonial-style houses and commemorates Queen Elizabeth II’s coronation with four Flame and Forest trees planted by various communities. A granite slab in the park has historical significance, related to Labuan's handover ceremony by Brunei.

Labuan Marine Museum

Opened in 2003, the Labuan Marine Museum aims to preserve and showcase Labuan Island's rich marine life. The museum is organized into 16 categories, covering a broad spectrum from general marine life to local fishing activities. It features exhibits on sea corals, various fish species, reptiles, sea plants, and artifacts from shipwrecks. A notable highlight is the replica skeleton of an Indian Fin Whale. The museum provides valuable insights into Labuan's marine environment and its historical significance.

Labuan Bird Park

Spanning 6.9 hectares, Labuan Bird Park is home to around 580 species of Borneo's unique birds, including Cockatoos, kingfishers, peacocks, hornbills, and sharmas. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as sightseeing, photographing the birds, and watching live bird shows. The park is suitable for all ages and offers an affordable entrance fee: RM3 for adults, RM1 for children aged 5 to 12, and free for those under 5. Open daily from 10 am to 5 pm, including public holidays, Labuan Bird Park promises an enjoyable and educational experience for the whole family.

Labuan Botanical Garden

The famous Botanical Gardens, once the grounds of the Government House known as 'Bumbung 12,' boasts stunning landscapes and ancient trees. The original Government House, notable for its unique roof design, was destroyed during World War II, leaving behind only a water storage tank. The park features historical elements, including two of Labuan Island's oldest graves, dating back to 1847, and a small grave for a foxhound named 'Jim.' Visitors can explore a hibiscus garden, a herbal garden, a manmade lake with an arched bridge, and a reflexology pathway, making it a delightful destination for both locals and tourists.

Water Village

Labuan’s water villages, primarily inhabited by Brunei Malays, are built on stilts over the water and connected by wooden walkways. These traditional homes, often with their own boats, offer a glimpse into local life. Visitors are welcome to explore the village, where friendly residents may offer local treats. The village features not only wooden houses and small shops but also basic amenities like seafood restaurants and a boardwalk. Additionally, tourists can enjoy various water sports activities in this unique setting.

Turtle Garden Resort

The Turtle Garden in Labuan is a conservation center dedicated to protecting sea turtles. Visitors can learn about various turtle species, their life cycles, and conservation efforts. The garden also rehabilitates injured turtles and educates the public about marine conservation.

World War II Memorial

The World War II Memorial on Labuan Island is a poignant tribute to soldiers who sacrificed their lives in the war, including Australians, British, Punjabis, locals, and New Zealanders. With meticulously maintained lawns and rows of plaques, the memorial honors 3,908 soldiers, of whom 2,156 remain unidentified. Each soldier is commemorated with a white stone bearing their name, military rank, and age at death. The memorial hosts a Remembrance Day service every first Sunday in November, drawing both locals and visitors. It’s a place for reflection and appreciation of the sacrifices made for peace.

Peace Park

The Peace Park on Labuan Island is a significant monument dedicated to peace and the bond between Japan and Malaysia. Built by the Japanese to atone for World War II, it honors those who lost their lives and symbolizes the renunciation of war. The park features a prominent man-made mound with a commemorative plaque. Nearby, the Japanese Surrender Point marks where Japanese forces officially surrendered to the Australians, led by Lieutenant General Masao Baba and Major General George Wootten, ending World War II in Borneo. It also hosted the first war crime trials in Southeast Asia.

Japanese Surrender Point

The Japanese Surrender Point on Labuan Island is a significant historical site where Japan officially surrendered to Australia, marking the end of World War II in Borneo. During the ceremony, Lieutenant General Masao Baba handed his sword to Major General George Wootten, symbolizing the handover. Not far from the Surrender Point is the Peace Park, built by the Japanese as a tribute to those who sacrificed their lives in the war. The park symbolizes peace and the enduring friendship between Malaysia and Japan. Additionally, the Surrender Point was also the site of Southeast Asia’s first war crimes trials.

State Mosque

The Labuan State Mosque, also known as the An'Nur Jamek Mosque, is renowned for its stunning, futuristic architecture that draws visitors from around the world. The mosque's design is so captivating that many tourists take time to admire and photograph its exterior. For those interested in exploring Malaysia's multicultural landscape, Labuan offers several other places of worship. The Kwong Fook Kung Temple, built in 1952, is the island's oldest Chinese temple and a popular spot among visitors. Additionally, the Sikh Temple, inspired by the Golden Temple in Amritsar, showcases impressive architecture and serves as a worship place for the Sikh community.
