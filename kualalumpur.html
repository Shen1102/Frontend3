About Information

Kuala Lumpur is one of three Federal Territories of Malaysia, enclaved within the state of Selangor, on the central west coast of Peninsular Malaysia. Since the 1990s, the city has played host to many international sporting, political and cultural events including the 1998 Commonwealth Games. Kuala Lumpur has undergone rapid development in recent decades, and is now the eighth most visited city in the world.

Kuala Lumpur Tower

The award-winning Kuala Lumpur Tower (KL Tower) is located at the top of Bukit Nanas in Kuala Lumpur. At 421 meters tall, it is the highest tower in Southeast Asia and the seventh tallest communication tower in the world. It’s one of the most iconic landmarks in Kuala Lumpur, offering a mix of cultural, adventure, and nature experiences.

Opened to the public in July 1996, KL Tower is built on Bukit Nanas Forest Reserve, the oldest protected forest area in Malaysia, which is home to ancient trees and local wildlife.

The tower's design features Islamic architecture with Arabic calligraphy, geometric tiles, and abstract patterns.

The indoor Observation Deck, 276 meters above ground, provides stunning views of the city. The outdoor Sky Deck, 300 meters up, offers even better views. The Sky Box, which extends from the Sky Deck with glass walls and floor, gives a unique perspective of the city.

Petronas Twin Tower

The Twin Towers are located in the heart of Kuala Lumpur, next to the famous Malaysian shopping center, Suria KLCC. They invite everyone, especially city residents, to experience their grandeur up close. Built in the 1990s, the towers reach a height of 452 meters (1,483 feet) and were the world's tallest buildings from 1998 to 2004.

One of the city’s most famous landmarks, the towers offer stunning views and are surrounded by great shopping and delicious food, making your visit truly memorable.

Suria KLCC shopping mall

If you love shopping, Suria KLCC is your paradise. It has many stores, restaurants, and entertainment options, offering everything from stylish fashion boutiques to traditional Malaysian crafts.

After a day of shopping at Suria KLCC, you can relax in KLCC Park, enjoy the green spaces and benches, and take in the views of the Twin Towers. You can also have a meal at the restaurants and cafes inside Suria KLCC. Book lovers will find a wide selection of books, from novels to nonfiction, at the Kinokuniya bookstore in Suria KLCC.

Saloma Bridge

On February 5, 2020, the beautiful Saloma Bridge opened to the public. This bridge, spanning the Klang River, honors Saloma, a famous Malaysian singer and actress known as the "Queen of Malay Cinema" in the late 1950s.

At night, the Saloma Bridge lights up in vibrant colors, creating a magical display over the river. It's a must-see spot that visitors will always remember.

Dataran Merdeka

Dataran Merdeka, or Independence Square, is a historic landmark in Kuala Lumpur where Malaysia declared its independence in 1957. Surrounded by well-preserved colonial architecture, including the Sultan Abdul Samad Building, the square features a large green field once used for British cricket games. Today, it hosts national celebrations and events like the Merdeka Parade, with its towering flagpole—one of the tallest in the world—symbolizing the nation's pride and sovereignty.

KL Forest Eco Park

Originally called Bukit Nanas Forest Reserve, this green space was set up as a reserve in 1906, making it one of the oldest conservation areas in the country. In the busy heart of Kuala Lumpur, you can find the charming KL Forest Eco Park, a green oasis amid the city’s hustle and bustle. The park covers 9.3 hectares and is home to a variety of wildlife, including Silver Leaf Monkeys and Long-Tailed Macaques that live comfortably here.

Besides its great location near the city center, KL Forest Eco Park offers activities and attractions for all ages. With well-maintained picnic areas and camping facilities, it’s a perfect spot for enjoying quality time with loved ones.

The park’s canopy walk is an amazing feature, with a wooden and steel bridge 21 meters high. This highlight of the park offers a 200-meter path leading to a great viewpoint where you can see some of the city’s oldest trees up close.

In addition to its impressive height, the canopy walk provides stunning views of the city. From this high vantage point, you can see the whole city of Kuala Lumpur and the iconic KL Tower. It’s a fantastic spot for photographers, offering endless chances to capture the beautiful contrast between the city and nature.

KL Bird Park

“The Kuala Lumpur Bird Park opened in November 1991 and is a popular eco-tourism spot. It features 3,000 colorful birds from over 200 species, both local and international. The park covers 20.9 acres and is next to the peaceful Kuala Lumpur Lake Gardens.

The Bird Park is known for its free-flight concept, which is the largest walk-in aviary in the world. Visitors can see birds flying freely around them. The park’s design and lush environment create many nesting sites, making it a great place for birds to breed.”

Perdana Botanical Garden

Perdana Botanical Garden, located in the center of Kuala Lumpur, is one of the city's largest and most popular parks. Covering 91.6 hectares, it attracts many locals and visitors. The garden, originally called Lake Gardens, was established in 1888 and renamed Perdana Botanical Garden in 1995.

This garden is special for its wide variety of plants from around the world. It features trees from Borneo, rare fruit collections, and a small herb garden. Informative signs next to the plants help you learn more about them. The park also has several beautiful lakes, making it a great spot for relaxing picnics and gatherings.

Orchid Garden

Inside the Perdana Botanical Garden, you'll find an orchid garden boasting a remarkable collection of over 3,000 orchid species, including rare and endangered varieties.

Sultan Abdul Samad Building

The Sultan Abdul Samad Building is one of Malaysia's most important and cherished heritage sites. Located in Kuala Lumpur, across from Dataran Merdeka (Independence Square) and the Royal Selangor Club, it was built in 1897 and named after the Sultan of Selangor at the time.

Designed by the British architect AC Norman, who also designed the Jamek Mosque and the National Textiles Museum, the building features a blend of British and Islamic styles. Its red brick structure includes three towers, tall white arches, a grand porch, curved colonnades, and two domes, with a 41.2-meter clock tower modeled after Big Ben.

Originally used by the British for administrative offices and known as Government Offices, it also served as Malaysia’s superior courts, including the Federal Court, the Court of Appeals, and the High Court of Malaya.

The building has witnessed many significant events, including the declaration of Malaysia’s independence on August 31, 1957, and the lowering of the British Union Jack. It also serves as the backdrop for Malaysia’s annual Independence Day parades, drawing large crowds of Malaysians and tourists.

Telekom Museum

Built in 1928, this building was originally used for manual telephone exchanges. In the early 1980s, it served as a storage site for telephone equipment in Kuala Lumpur.

In 1985, former Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad suggested turning the building into a national heritage site. Telekom Malaysia then converted it into a museum.

The museum's architecture features a distinct Neoclassical style, highlighted by eight iconic columns at one of its entrances. The building also includes Palladian elements, such as an open courtyard and archways.

Inside the museum, you can explore interesting displays, artifacts, and replicas that show the history of telecommunications in Malaysia. As you move through the different floors, you'll see a mix of historical and modern technology. Each room focuses on a different period or aspect of telecommunications, making the museum both educational and engaging.

Masjid Jamek

Known as one of the oldest mosques in Kuala Lumpur, Masjid Jamek is a must-visit spot in the area. Located between the Klang and Gombak rivers, it is a popular place for tourists.

Built in 1909 by Sultan Abdul Samad, Masjid Jamek is still the main mosque for the city's Muslim community. Its name comes from the Arabic word “Jami,” which means a place where the community gathers for worship.

Kuala Lumpur Railway Station

The Kuala Lumpur Railway Station, a blend of Eastern and Western architectural styles, is a historic landmark and a key transport hub in the city. Completed in the early 20th century, it served as Kuala Lumpur's main railway station before the opening of KL Sentral. Today, it remains a functioning station and a testament to Malaysia's colonial heritage and its journey to modernity. Visitors can enjoy both its historical significance and its role in the city's transportation network.
