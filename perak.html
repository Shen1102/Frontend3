About Information

Perak is an underrated travel spot known for its tropical beauty and rich cultural history. Once a British colony, it has grown into a bustling city with friendly locals since gaining independence. The city features many colonial buildings that have been updated to reflect a modern style. Along with its famous landmarks, Perak also boasts natural wonders, underwater treasures, and stunning sandy beaches.

Pangkor Island

Pangkor Island, located on the west coast of Peninsular Malaysia, is a hidden gem just a 10-minute ferry ride from the mainland. Known for its stunning beaches and relaxing atmosphere, the island features picturesque fishing villages, seafood processing factories offering treats like fish satay, and duty-free shopping. Key attractions include a 17th-century Dutch fort and beautiful beaches like Teluk Nipah, Coral Bay, and Pasir Bogak. Visitors can enjoy beach activities such as kayaking and snorkeling or unique experiences like hornbill feeding. The island also boasts distinctive mosques and Taoist temples.

The easiest way to reach Pangkor Island is by ferry from Lumut. The island offers a range of luxury accommodations, including Double Tree by Hilton Damai Laut, eco-friendly Alun Retreat, and the exclusive Pangkor Laut Resort. These options provide unforgettable stays with top-notch amenities and experiences.

Teluk Batik

Located approximately 7 km from Lumut Town and about 1.5 hours’ drive from Ipoh City, Teluk Batik is one of Perak's most renowned beaches. This picturesque stretch of fine sandy shore is a popular destination for beachgoers and families, offering a relaxing retreat and various recreational activities.

Tukun Island

Tukun Island, also known as Fairway Rock or Fairway White Rock, is a small island about 5.5 meters high, located approximately 4 miles SSW of Southeast Point on Pulau Pangkor. It can also be accessed from Pulau Pinang. The island is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving due to its vibrant marine life and clear waters.

Teluk Intan

Teluk Intan, formerly known as Teluk Anson, is renowned for its Leaning Tower, tranquil river cruises, and local cuisine. The Leaning Tower, initially built as a water tank in 1885, stands 25 meters tall and resembles Italy’s Leaning Tower of Pisa. It serves as both a clock tower and a historical landmark with eight stories and 110 steps. The tower’s vibrant pagoda-style architecture features white, red, and green colors. For a small fee of RM 2, visitors can explore its interior, which showcases Teluk Intan’s history and artifacts.

Birch Memorial Clock Tower

A must-visit in Ipoh is the Birch Memorial Clock Tower, opened in 1909 to honor James Birch, the first British resident of Perak, who was murdered in 1875. The tower features an air-conditioned staircase leading to a viewpoint offering panoramic views of Ipoh. On the ground level, enjoy the peaceful garden with intricate Malay wood carvings and a bench for relaxation. Interestingly, a street to the north is named after Dato Maharajalela, Birch's murderer.

New Agacia Town Kampar

If you’ve ever dreamed of visiting Disneyland, New Agacia Town Kampar is a must-see attraction in Perak. Designed to resemble Hong Kong Disneyland’s Main Street, this charming spot features pastel-colored buildings that make for fantastic photo opportunities. In addition to Disney-themed cafes, you'll find a replica of Hollywood's famous Walk of Fame, adding an extra touch of magic to your visit.

Taiping Clock Tower

The Taiping Clock Tower, Malaysia's oldest, is a historic landmark rebuilt in 1890 from its original wooden design. Standing 20 meters tall, it features a brick pyramid roof with precision-cut arches and a rooster weathervane. The tower's open balcony and decorative flags add to its charm. Serving as a tourist information center, it provides maps and details about Taiping, though there's no access to the top. The tower remains a striking sight and a key feature in Taiping's town center.

Tasik Cermin

Escape the hustle and bustle of city life and visit Tasik Cermin in Ipoh, a serene lake nestled among majestic limestone hills. Known for its stunning mirror-like reflection, Tasik Cermin offers a tranquil experience with boat rides through tunnels and breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and trees. The lake is divided into two main areas: Tasik Cermin 1, which requires walking through a 90-meter mining tunnel to reach, and Tasik Cermin 2, accessible only by a 130-meter-long water tunnel, showcasing its hidden beauty.

Aside from the mesmerizing lakes, Tasik Cermin features several attractions such as the Monkey Swing Playground, the colorful Rocky Sky Ladder, and the Snake Mouth Cave. Visitors can also enjoy local snacks at Kafe Lake View while taking in the scenic surroundings. The best time to visit is in the morning when the reflections are most vivid, but the sunset cruise also offers a magical view with flocks of birds and shimmering fireflies.

Tempurung Cave

Tempurung Cave, located in Gopeng, Perak, is one of the largest and longest caves in Peninsular Malaysia, estimated to be around 400 million years old. The cave stretches 4.5 kilometers, with 1.9 kilometers open to visitors, and features a 1.6-kilometer-long underground river. It is divided into five distinct domes, each showcasing unique stalactites, stalagmites, and cave formations. Highlights include the Golden Flowstone Cavern, Alam Cavern, Tin Mine Cavern, Gergasi Cavern, and Battlefield Cavern, each offering different natural wonders and historical remnants.

Visitors can choose between dry and wet adventure tours. The dry tours involve climbing over 600 steps to explore the cave’s formations, including unique stalactites and the Golden Flowstone. The wet tours are more adventurous, involving wading through the underground river, jumping, crawling, and sliding, typically taking two to three hours to complete. Tempurung Cave is ideal for both thrill-seekers and nature enthusiasts looking for an adventurous experience.

Taiping Lake Gardens

Taiping Lake Gardens, established in 1884 on the site of abandoned tin mines, is Malaysia's first public park and remains one of its most picturesque locations. Funded by wealthy tin miner Chung Keng Quee and designed by town planner Charles Compton Reade, the park features expansive lakes, lush greenery, and majestic rain trees that create a serene atmosphere. The park includes West Lake, South Lake, Jungle Lake, and several smaller lakes and ponds linked by scenic footpaths and decorative bridges, many of which are illuminated at night.

The park seamlessly integrates with the town of Taiping, offering a peaceful retreat with views of Bukit Larut in the background. Visitors enjoy various activities, including paddle boating, bike rentals, and a playground for children. The gardens are a popular spot for morning and late afternoon exercise, and their open design allows easy access to nearby attractions like Taiping Zoo and the War Cemetery.

Blue Tears

Experience the mesmerizing Blue Tears phenomenon at Sembilan Island, where the sea water lights up in vibrant neon blue with any movement. This stunning glow, visible near the shore, is caused by rare bioluminescent plankton, creating a magical night-time spectacle. For an unforgettable view of this natural wonder, be sure to book a local tour.

Museum Perak

Perak Museum, Malaysia's first museum, was founded by Sir Hugh Low in 1883 and completed in stages, with the main building finished in 1886 and the additional wings in 1889. The second floor was added in 1900 to expand its collections. The museum is open from Tuesday to Sunday, from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

The museum features diverse collections, starting with a zoology section displaying taxidermy specimens of various animals, including tapirs and other exotic wildlife. Another section focuses on Malaysia's diverse cultures, showcasing traditional artifacts, pottery, and cultural practices from different ethnic groups. The second floor highlights Orang Asli artefacts, including unique masks, baskets, mannequins, and traditional musical instruments, offering a deep dive into the craftsmanship and heritage of Malaysia's indigenous people.

Perak Tong Cave Temple

Perak Cave Temple, located in Gunung Tasek, Ipoh, is built within a natural cave and is renowned for its towering golden Buddha statue, over 40 feet tall, surrounded by four guardian deities. Inside the temple, 358 steps behind the main altar lead to various sections of the cave, showcasing different Buddhist statues and elegant pavilions with scenic views. Visitors can also climb 450 steps to the top of the limestone hill for a panoramic view of the city. The temple features vibrant murals on its walls that narrate stories of the Chinese community and Buddhist scriptures.

Royal Town

The Royal Town of Kuala Kangsar is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and traditional Malay crafts, including exquisite pottery and architecture. This charming city offers an authentic taste of local cuisine, like the unique laksa telur sarang, which features a crispy egg yolk omelette. Notably, Kuala Kangsar is home to Malaysia’s first rubber tree, planted in 1877 by botanist Henry Nicholas Ridley and still standing today.

Key attractions in Kuala Kangsar include the stunning Ubudiah Mosque, known for its golden domes and Italian marble floors, designed by Arthur Benison Hubback. Nearby, Istana Kenangan (Palace of Dreams), built in 1931, showcases traditional Malay wooden architecture and has served various royal functions. Now a museum, Istana Kenangan offers insights into the region’s royal history and craftsmanship.

Ipoh Railway Station

Originally constructed as a hospital, the station in Ipoh was later converted into a railway hub and is now one of the city's prominent landmarks. Resembling a miniature Taj Mahal, the station has long served both locals and travelers, offering transportation, hotel rooms, a bar, and a restaurant. Today, the Majestic Hotel, located within the station, provides a convenient place for weary visitors to refresh after their journey.
