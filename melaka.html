About Information

Melaka, also known as Malacca, is located in the southern part of Malaysia and is a popular destination for travelers. Founded by the Portuguese, ruled by the Dutch, and later controlled by the British, it boasts a rich blend of cultures and a colorful history. If you're seeking a unique cultural experience in Malaysia, Melaka is the perfect choice.

Today, it is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage City.

Come and experience the wonder and excitement of historic Melaka!

Pantai Klebang

For a relaxing escape into nature, head to Pantai Klebang. This tranquil beach, surrounded by shady trees, offers a peaceful retreat where you can enjoy the sun and calm atmosphere. Rent chairs and tables for added comfort, and visit closer to sunset for stunning views of the sun setting over the water. Exciting activities include horseback riding along the beach, ATV rides, and entertainment for children with kites and bubbles.

Feeling hungry? The beach is lined with food stalls offering a variety of local treats, including Melaka’s famous kuih keria and refreshing coconut shakes. Pantai Klebang is easily accessible with a 20-minute drive from Melaka Airport, ample parking, or bus number 503. Nearby attractions include the Melaka Submarine Museum and Baba Charlie Nyonya Cafe, which serves delicious Nyonya kuih just three kilometers from the beach.

Tanjung Kling

Tanjung Kling, located about 15 km from Malacca Town, features a beautiful white-sand beach. Nearby hotels provide a variety of activities, including windsurfing, kayaking, banana boat rides, scuba diving lessons, and island-hopping tours.

Tanjung Bidara

Tanjung Bidara, located about 20 km from Malacca Town, offers a serene beach atmosphere surrounded by lush vegetation. It’s an ideal spot for relaxation and recreational activities. The beach features a variety of hawker stalls selling local food, and amenities such as parking lots, playgrounds, and showers are also available.

Mamee Jonker House

As you explore Jonker Street, make sure to visit the vibrant Mamee Jonker House, marked by its cheerful yellow facade and large Mamee signage. This family-friendly attraction is perfect for fans of the nostalgic snack. Inside, you'll find a café, a mini-museum, and a shop with Mamee-themed souvenirs and merchandise. Don't miss the chance to customize your own Mamee cup noodles in their interactive kitchen.

Freeport A'Famosa Outlet

Freeport A’Famosa Outlet in Melaka offers a distinctive shopping experience with its Dutch-inspired design and over 80 stores. Located half an hour from Melaka city center, this shopping haven includes a range of upper-middle to high-end brands across 180,000 square feet. Visitors can enjoy attractions like five water features, a lake, parks, and a grand windmill, perfect for photo opportunities.

A highlight of the outlet is Coach Airways, a unique store set inside a remodeled 1981 Boeing 747. This exclusive shop showcases Coach products in a luxurious setting and features a photo-friendly front section, a spacious product area, and a café offering complimentary drinks with purchases. The outlet also offers local dining options and fun activities, such as pony feeding at the nearby zoo.

A'Famosa Water Park

Heading to Melaka with friends or family? Don’t miss out on the thrilling fun at the A' Famosa Water Park! This aquatic playground offers hours of entertainment for both adults and kids. Enjoy a variety of attractions including game pools, twisting water slides, and water playgrounds for little ones. Plus, the features one of Southeast Asia's tallest, longest, and widest 'ice cream' rides!

Menara Taming Sari

For a panoramic view of Melaka, take a ride in a glass cabin up the Menara Taming Sari, an iconic gyro tower with a revolving observatory that reaches 110 meters above the ground. From the top, you'll enjoy a 360-degree view of the city, spotting landmarks like Pulau Melaka, the Maritime Museum, and the Melaka River. For a different experience, visit in the evening or at night to see the city illuminated by lights.

Zoo Melaka

Zoo Melaka, spanning 50 acres in Ayer Keroh, is home to around 450 wildlife species, including the Malayan Tiger and Malayan Tapir. The zoo, open daily from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM on weekdays and 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on weekends, features spacious enclosures where animals roam freely, offering an immersive nature experience.

Visitors can enjoy interactive activities such as feeding friendly elephants with sugarcane, and exploring the colorful bird aviary with vibrant parrots and majestic peacocks. The petting section also provides opportunities to ride or feed ponies, with feeding times scheduled from 12:00 PM to 12:30 PM and 2:30 PM to 3:00 PM.

Melaka River Cruise

Experience the charm of Melaka with a cruise along the Melaka River. Operating daily from 9:00 AM to 11:00 PM, the Melaka River Cruise offers daytime and nighttime options to enjoy the city's vibrant sights. As you float past lively restaurants, bars, and historic landmarks like the Tan Kim Seng Bridge and Kampung Jawa Bridge, you’ll gain insights into Melaka’s rich culture and history.

Enhance your cruise with special promotions such as the Dinner Cruise, featuring a selection of delicious Melakan dishes, or the VIP Cruise, available Friday to Sunday evenings, offering a more personalized experience.

Skytrex Adventure Melaka

Looking for an adrenaline-packed adventure? Head to Skytrex Adventure Melaka, where you can tackle a range of thrilling obstacle courses. Experience Malaysia's first Skybike, take a leap on the Tarzan Bungee Swing, soar on the Flying Fox, and much more! With various difficulty levels suitable for all ages and fitness levels, it's an ideal spot for family outings or group adventures with friends.

A'Famosa Safari Wonderland

Animal enthusiasts will love the A'Famosa Safari Wonderland at A'Famosa Resort. This expansive safari-style zoo lets you get close to towering giraffes, majestic tigers, elegant flamingos, cheeky squirrel monkeys, playful primates, and more. Enjoy a range of daily activities, including multi-animal shows, feeding sessions, and engaging performances. Perfect for families and kids, this zoo offers an exciting and educational experience you won’t want to miss.

Dutch Square

During your day trip in Melaka, a visit to the iconic Dutch Square is a must. This historic area is filled with striking red buildings that reflect Melaka's deep history. The square is situated just a short three-minute walk from the bustling Jonker Street, or you can take the Melaka Bus Town No. 17, which will drop you nearby for a brief stroll to the square.

Dutch Square is home to several notable landmarks. The Melaka Clock Tower, standing 50 feet tall, has been a central feature of the town for over 134 years. Built in 1886 by Tan Jiak Kim to honor his father Tan Beng Swee, the clock tower continues to chime every hour. Nearby, the Queen Victoria’s Fountain, erected in 1901 to commemorate Queen Victoria’s Diamond Jubilee, remains a symbol of British colonial influence. The Stadthuys, the oldest Dutch building in the East, now houses The History and Ethnography Museum, showcasing Melaka’s rich historical and cultural heritage.

Jonker Street

Jonker Street, a vibrant hub in Melaka, offers a lively experience with its bustling atmosphere and rich local charm. The street is famous for its 200-meter-long dragon replica, which symbolizes Melaka's grandeur and unity. Visitors can explore a variety of shops offering souvenirs, snacks, and unique gifts. The street is also renowned for its street food, where you can savor authentic Baba Nyonya cuisine and other Malaysian delights.

On weekends, the Jonker Street Night Market comes alive with countless stalls selling food and knick-knacks from 6:00 PM to midnight. Nearby, the Jonker Walk World Heritage Park provides a peaceful retreat with its tiled pathways and a statue of Malaysian bodybuilder Gan Boon Leong. Conveniently located just five minutes from Dutch Square, Jonker Street is easily accessible by car or bus, with musical trishaws offering a fun mode of transport around Melaka.

The Church of St Paul

At the top of St. Paul’s Hill, visitors are greeted by the remarkable ruins of the Church of St. Paul, a site steeped in history. Originally built in 1521 by Portuguese Captain Duarte Coelho, the church was known as the Chapel of Mother of God. It served as a Catholic place of worship until the Dutch took over Melaka and the church was replaced by Christ Church.

Today, the Church of St. Paul is recognized as Malaysia’s oldest Catholic church and a significant Southeast Asian landmark. The site features a statue of St. Francis Xavier, who was once buried here, and an open grave covered by a metal cage, allowing visitors a glimpse into its historic depth. The church's walls also display old Dutch tombstones, adding to its historical allure.

A Famosa

A Famosa is a historic Portuguese fortress in Melaka. During your visit, explore its five-storey keep, the ammunition storage room, the captain’s residence, and the officer’s quarters to delve into its rich history.
