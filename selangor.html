About Information

Selangor, Malaysia's most developed and wealthiest state, plays a major role in the country's industry while still preserving several parks and forest reserves. Despite industrialization in some areas, Selangor offers a range of attractions including snow parks, theme parks, zoos, and beautiful beaches.

For those interested in history and culture, Selangor boasts a variety of historical monuments such as mosques, temples, and museums. Additionally, the state features scenic mountains that provide stunning panoramic views, making it a diverse destination for both leisure and exploration.

Kelanang Beach

Discover the charm of Kelanang Beach, located near Morib Beach. This unique spot offers breathtaking views of the Straits of Melaka, framed by mangroves and ru trees. It's the perfect place to enjoy stunning sunsets and a refreshing breeze, ideal for flying kites. Families will appreciate the on-site playground, providing a fun and safe space for children to enjoy.

Cunang Beach

Cunang Beach, a relatively hidden gem in Tanjung Sepat, is renowned for its stunning white sand that stretches alongside mangroves. Managed by the indigenous Mah Meri community, it has recently become a popular tourist destination in Selangor. Visitors can enjoy the beach's picturesque setting, complete with traditional houses, vibrant decorations, and local handicraft shops. The beach also offers a range of activities, including fishing and cycling, making it a delightful spot for both relaxation and adventure.

Remis Beach

Remis Beach, a 20-kilometer stretch in the Jeram mukim of Kuala Selangor’s Southern District, has recently reopened following Selangor’s entry into Phase 3. Known as a popular eco-tourism spot, it attracts many visitors who flock to enjoy fresh seafood, including grilled fish and boiled or grilled mussels, while relaxing with family and friends. The beach’s vibrant atmosphere and delicious culinary offerings make it a favored destination for both locals and tourists.

i-City Theme Park

i-City, a dazzling 25-acre attraction in Shah Alam, Malaysia, is renowned as one of the world's Top 25 brightly-lit places. Since its opening in December 2009, it has captivated visitors with its City of Digital Lights, featuring over 1 million LED light displays that create a vibrant, colorful experience. Highlights include Snowalk, a 50,000-square-foot arctic adventure kept below 5 degrees Celsius, and WaterWorld, home to Southeast Asia's first Tornado Ride.

For additional fun, explore FunWorld, which houses Malaysia's first interactive wax museum, The Red Carpet, and the Trick Art Museum. Whether you're enjoying thrilling rides, taking in stunning light displays, or engaging with unique exhibits, i-City offers a memorable experience for the whole family.

Sunway Lagoon Theme Park

Sunway Lagoon Theme Park, a long-time favorite, offers an array of adventures designed to thrill visitors of all ages. Spanning 88 acres, this expansive park features over 90 attractions divided into distinct zones, including the Water Park, Amusement Park, Wildlife Park, XPark, Sunway Lost Lagoon, and Scream Park. With its mix of exhilarating water slides and spine-chilling haunted houses, Sunway Lagoon promises an action-packed experience.

A unique feature of Sunway Lagoon is its dual water parks. The Sunway Lost Lagoon, nestled within lush greenery, emulates a rainforest paradise with its massive water rides, playgrounds, and stunning waterfalls. Visitors can also enjoy various dining options throughout the park, ensuring a satisfying end to a fun-filled day.

Skytrex

Located in the scenic and hilly region of Hulu Langat, Skytrex is the ultimate destination for adventure enthusiasts. Nestled amidst rivers and waterfalls, this park offers a thrilling array of activities, including a flying fox, step bridges, and monkey bars, all set against a stunning natural backdrop.

In addition to its exhilarating attractions, Skytrex features food and fruit stalls for a quick snack during your adventure. Families can also enjoy picnic areas around the park, providing a peaceful spot to relax and take in the serene environment.

Zoo Negara

Spend a day immersed in nature and wildlife at Zoo Negara, Malaysia’s largest zoo, which spans over 100 acres and houses more than 3,472 animals across 301 species. Open daily from 9 AM to 5 PM, with last admission at 4 PM, the zoo features spacious, open-concept exhibits where you can observe animals roaming freely. Located just 5 kilometers from Kuala Lumpur’s city center, it’s easily accessible via LRT and Rapid KL buses.

Explore over 12 sections, including the Giant Panda Conservation Centre with Malaysia's first pandas, an extensive aquarium showcasing the Malaysian river system, and a vibrant Bird Aviary. Discover the reptile house with its impressive collection of the world’s largest crocodiles and longest snakes. Enjoy educational shows, interactive talks, and tram rides, and take advantage of the zoo's facilities for hosting events like birthdays and weddings.

Sekinchan

Sekinchan, a charming coastal town in Selangor's Sabak Bernam district, is renowned for its breathtaking paddy field vistas that extend up to the Perak border. The town's picturesque landscapes, with fields that shift from lush green to golden brown depending on the season, make for stunning photo opportunities. A visit to the Paddy Gallery offers a fascinating look into rice production and the opportunity to purchase fresh rice directly from the source.

Sky Mirror

Sky Mirror, located in Kuala Selangor about 1.74 nautical miles off Jeram, is renowned for its breathtaking mirror-like reflections of the sky, reminiscent of Bolivia's Salar de Uyuni. This enchanting sandbank island emerges only during low tide, typically for a few hours in the morning, offering a rare and mesmerizing photographic opportunity. The sandbank appears around the 1st and 15th of each month, according to the Chinese lunar calendar, as well as the days before and after these dates.

To visit Sky Mirror, you’ll need to join a guided tour, which lasts about four hours and includes a 45 to 60-minute stay on the sandbank. The experience is weather and tide-dependent, with the best conditions occurring when the water is just an inch above the sand. While there, you can also explore the marine life such as soldier crabs, hermit crabs, sea anemones, and bamboo clams, adding to the unique allure of this natural phenomenon.

Broga Hill

Broga Hill, a favorite among hikers, offers a rewarding weekend escape with its 400-meter height and stunning aerial views. The hike features two trails: one shorter but steeper, and the other longer with a more gradual ascent. The trails are well-marked, leading to three viewing points at 880 feet, 1150 feet, and the summit at 1312 feet. Many hikers start early to catch the sunrise from the top, where they’re greeted by vast grassy expanses and panoramic views. Remember to bring a torchlight and wear appropriate footwear for a comfortable trek.

For those looking to extend their adventure, Paradise Valley Resort near Broga Hill provides a variety of recreational activities including flying fox, camping, kayaking, and rock climbing. The resort also offers fruit farms where visitors can sample tropical delights like jackfruit and passion fruit. Whether you're planning a fun family holiday, a romantic getaway, or a corporate retreat, Paradise Valley Resort is an excellent destination to enjoy both nature and a range of activities.

Selangor Fruits Valley

If you're planning a weekend outing with family and friends, Selangor Fruits Valley (SFV) in Batang Berjuntai, Rawang, is the perfect destination. Spanning approximately 2,000 acres, SFV features a diverse range of attractions, including fruit trees, an apiary, a rubber plantation, lakes, campsites, an orchid farm, and even a biking trail. Visitors can enjoy interactive activities such as feeding animals at the deer farm, exploring a mini petting zoo, kayaking, and experiencing traditional kampung life at Rumah Kampung, complete with a chicken coop and fresh coconut stalls.

SFV is also renowned for its extensive fruit cultivation, including Malaysia’s largest star fruit farm and 20 other fruit varieties like mangoes and rambutans. Don’t miss the chance to try their unique fruit hybrid, the Nangchem, a delightful jackfruit and cempedak crossbreed. With its blend of educational and recreational activities, SFV offers a fun and immersive experience for fruit lovers and families alike.

Batu Caves

Batu Caves, located in Gombak, Selangor, is a must-visit destination known for its stunning 272 colorful steps and impressive Hindu temple. These ancient limestone caves, formed over 400 million years ago, are home to a magnificent temple built in 1920. The site features the towering golden statue of Lord Murugan, a prominent symbol of the Hindu faith, standing at 140 feet tall. Visitors are drawn to Batu Caves not only for its architectural marvels but also for the vibrant Thaipusam festival, which brings together thousands of Hindus in celebration.

The journey to Batu Caves involves climbing the vibrant steps, which offer breathtaking views of the surrounding greenery. For a quieter visit, arrive before 8:00 AM to enjoy the serene atmosphere. Be prepared for encounters with local monkeys and explore additional attractions like the psychedelic Ramanya Cave, the scenic Cave Villa, and the expansive Dark Cave, which offers guided tours for a deeper experience of its natural wonders.

Klang Heritage Walk

Klang, a historic town in Selangor, offers a rich tapestry of heritage buildings that reflect its deep cultural and historical roots. Established as a prominent trading hub and once the royal capital of Malaysia, Klang features notable sites such as the Royal Gallery (Galeri Diraja Sultan Abdul Aziz), showcasing royal artifacts and historical memorabilia. Other significant landmarks include Chennai Silk, an old bank turned famous saree shop, and the Royal Klang Club, renowned for its marine-themed interior and historical significance. The Church of Our Lady of Lourdes, built in 1923, and the Klang Fire Station, a Victorian-era building, further highlight Klang's historical depth.

Tengku Kelana Street, known as Little India, offers a vibrant experience with its array of Indian shops and dining options, while the Klang Fire Station provides insights into historical firefighting methods. Each site in Klang tells a unique story, making it a must-visit for those interested in Malaysia's heritage. For a comprehensive exploration, consider joining a local tour led by Tourism Selangor.

Mah Meri Cultural Village

Nestled on Carey Island, the Mah Meri Cultural Village offers an immersive experience into the traditional life of the Mah Meri tribe, known as the "Masked Men of Malaysia." Established in 2011, this cultural village provides visitors with a unique opportunity to explore and engage with the Mah Meri way of life, including their customs, food, and vibrant celebrations. The village highlights the tribe's rich heritage through interactive activities that showcase their traditional practices and artistic expressions.

The Mah Meri tribe settled on Carey Island over a century ago, and the cultural village was created to preserve and share their rich heritage. By offering employment opportunities and showcasing their rituals and arts, the village helps sustain the tribe’s cultural legacy while allowing visitors to gain a deeper understanding of their unique traditions and daily life.

Muzium Orang Asli Gombak

Situated within Selangor State Park, Gombak’s Orang Asli Museum showcases the rich culture and traditions of the indigenous Orang Asli people of Peninsular Malaysia.
