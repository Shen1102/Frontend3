About Information

Kedah, one of Malaysia's oldest states, is renowned for its serene rural landscapes and is often called "the rice bowl of Malaysia" due to its extensive paddy fields. The state’s economy relies heavily on agriculture, particularly rice cultivation. With its diverse natural scenery, including plains, forests, islands, and mountains, Kedah offers a range of outdoor activities such as mountain trekking, rafting, and nature adventures. Visitors can explore hot springs, waterfalls, and expansive rice fields, or unwind on its beautiful beaches and islands.

Langkawi

Langkawi, an island archipelago surrounded by inviting turquoise waters, is a duty-free paradise. With postcard-perfect sunset views from its beaches, it’s no wonder Langkawi is a top attraction in Kedah. The island boasts excellent diving sites and numerous attractions, including Underwater World Langkawi, Pantai Cenang Beach, and the Sky Bridge in Pantai Kok, where you can walk among the mountaintops. T o fully explore the island, consider renting a vehicle and make sure to allocate time for duty-free shopping at the malls.

Pulau Payar Marine Park

Pulau Payar Marine Park, Malaysia’s oldest marine sanctuary, is a prime spot for snorkeling and diving. Comprising four uninhabited islands—Pulau Kaca, Pulau Lembu, Pulau Segantang, and the largest, Pulau Payar—the park is renowned for its diverse coral reefs and vibrant marine life, including barracudas, clownfish, and black-tipped reef sharks. Highlights include the Coral Garden, a top diving site with stunning coral formations, and an observation platform for those who prefer to stay dry. Visitors can also enjoy swimming, baby shark feeding, and hiking trails within the park.

Despite being uninhabited, Pulau Payar Marine Park is equipped with amenities like picnic tables, barbecue pits, gazebos, and restrooms, making it a family-friendly destination. As a conservation area, the park emphasizes ecotourism and encourages visitors to respect the environment by not removing marine organisms or engaging in fishing. During baby shark feeding sessions, maintaining a respectful distance from the sharks is also advised.

Cenang Beach

Cenang Beach is the heart of Langkawi Island, bustling with activity along its vibrant shoreline. The area is dotted with shops, restaurants, cafes, and pubs, offering everything from swimsuits and floats to chocolates and souvenirs. For a delightful meal, you’ll find some of Langkawi’s finest seafood restaurants right here, making it an ideal spot for both shopping and dining.

Alor Setar Tower

The Alor Setar Tower, one of Malaysia's most iconic structures, stands out prominently in the city of Alor Setar. Its observation deck, perched 88 meters above ground, offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city. You can enjoy stunning sunset vistas on the horizon and then savor a meal at the revolving restaurant for an unforgettable dining experience with a constantly changing view.

DR. Mahathir’s Mohammad Birth Place Complex

Tun Dr. Mahathir Mohamad, Malaysia’s fourth and longest-serving Prime Minister, was born in this traditional one-room wooden house in 1925. The museum offers a deep dive into his life and achievements, featuring exhibits such as period furniture, family genealogy documents, and school photographs. The grounds also include two exhibition halls, an auditorium, an office, a souvenir shop, and the first ice factory in Alor Setar.

Nobat Hall

The Nobat Hall is a distinctive three-story octagonal building designed to house the Nobat musical instruments, some of Malaysia’s oldest and most significant musical artifacts. These traditional instruments are used in major ceremonial events, including royal coronations, funerals, and other official functions. Originally constructed from timber, the hall was restored with stone and metal by the late Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah.

MnM Home Whimsical Houses

This whimsical location combines the charm of "Alice in Wonderland" with the fantasy of "The Hobbit." The uniquely designed houses and structures, including a Hobbit's hole, an upside-down house, and the Twin Leaning House, create a playful and surreal atmosphere. It’s a perfect spot for capturing intriguing and fun photographs, making it a must-visit attraction in Kedah for those seeking a memorable visual experience.

Kilim River Cruise

A highlight of the Kilim River cruise is the chance to observe the nocturnal bats in the Bat Cave. Visitors can witness both megabats, which are large fruit-eaters, and microbats, including Vampire Bats that feed on insects and blood. This unique experience offers a fascinating glimpse into the daily lives of these creatures as they fly, sleep, and feed.

The cruise also features exciting activities like eagle feeding at prime bird-watching spots. Here, you can see various eagle species, including the white-bellied fish eagle, sea eagles, and Brahminy kite, especially during their migratory seasons in September and March. Additionally, cruising through the Hole in the Wall—a narrow gap between limestone cliffs—transports you to the Andaman Sea, where you can enjoy fresh meals from local fish farms at a floating restaurant.

Sungai Merbok

Spread over 4,000 hectares, the mangrove forest around Sungai Merbok in Kedah is a paradise for nature lovers and photographers. This serene ecosystem showcases remarkable natural spectacles, including flocks of Little Egrets, kingfishers, and waders. Mangrove forests, which are crucial for their biodiversity and protection of coastal areas, are richly represented here with 40 of the world’s 60 mangrove species. Visitors can explore this ecological haven from the Semeling Jetty Complex, which houses the Mangrove Forest Reserve Museum and a Tourist Information Centre. A boardwalk allows for a scenic stroll through the mangrove swamps, enhancing the experience of this unique environment.

Top Walk Sungai Sedim

Malaysia boasts the world's longest rainforest canopy walk at Tree Top Walk Sungai Sedim, located about 30 minutes from Kulim, Kedah. Spanning 925 meters, this elevated walk offers a unique chance to experience breathtaking bird’s-eye views of the lush rainforest while remaining 26 meters above the forest floor. The walk is self-supporting, ensuring that no trees are harmed.

In addition to the canopy walk, Sungai Sedim Recreational Park provides a range of activities to cater to all interests, from thrilling sports for adrenaline seekers to educational and relaxing experiences. Whether you're looking for a scenic stroll or an adventurous day out, Tree Top Walk Sungai Sedim has something for everyone.

Lata Bayu

Lata Bayu is a popular waterfall destination in Kedah, frequented by locals, especially on weekends. Set amidst lush greenery, it offers a serene environment perfect for relaxation. The park is well-maintained and equipped with various amenities for visitor comfort. It's also an ideal spot for families, as the shallow ponds provide a safe and enjoyable setting for children to splash around in the evening.

Zahir Mosque

Built in 1912, Zahir Mosque (Masjid Zahir) in Alor Setar, Kedah, is one of Malaysia's oldest and most stunning mosques, celebrated as one of the world’s top 10 most beautiful mosques. This architectural marvel, which serves as a key icon of the state, features striking black domes—the main dome surrounded by five smaller ones symbolizing the five pillars of Islam. Inspired by the Azizi Mosque in North Sumatera, the mosque's design includes intricately detailed columns, ornamented arches, and a prayer hall adorned with Quranic verses.

Situated on the site of a former cemetery for Kedah warriors, Zahir Mosque has maintained its charm through only two major renovations, in 1960 and 1975. Visitors are drawn to its serene atmosphere, enhanced by a wide verandah walkway surrounding the prayer hall. To enter, visitors must dress modestly, with shoulders and knees covered, and women are encouraged to wear a headscarf.

Masjid al-Badr-Floating Mosque

Located on the tax-free Pangkor Island in Perak, Malaysia, Masjid Al-Badr Seribu Selawat is a striking floating mosque renowned for its stunning architecture influenced by Turkish and Middle Eastern designs. Built on March 7, 2014, this mosque features a vivid blue dome adorned with gold-like mosaics and intricate geometric patterns that reflect the cultural heritage of Perak. The mosque can accommodate about 1,500 people and is distinguished by its decorative elements, including flower-shaped carvings and khat script, which create a serene and tranquil atmosphere.

Visitors can reach Pangkor Island by taking a bus from Kuala Lumpur to Lumut, followed by a 30-minute ferry ride. In addition to exploring the mosque, the island offers various activities such as snorkeling, jet-skiing, and kayaking at Coral Island, as well as opportunities to observe hornbills at Nipah Bay and visit the historical Dutch fortress at Kampung Teluk Gedung.

Kedah Paddy Museum

The Paddy Museum, designed to resemble bundles of harvested rice stalks, features stunning murals and detailed exhibits on rice cultivation. Visitors can explore various rice varieties and gain insight into the essential role rice plays in the diets of over a billion people worldwide.

Wat Nikrodharam Temple

A key attraction in Alor Setar is the Wat Nikrodharam Temple, located between Telok Wanjah and Jalan Stadium. This temple beautifully blends Thai architectural elements with subtle Chinese motifs, reflecting a unique cross-cultural design. It is a site where both Theravada and Mahayana Buddhist traditions are practiced and honored.
