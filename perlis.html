About Information

With a history dating back to the early 1800s, Perlis is the northern gateway to Malaysia, bordered by Kedah to the south and Thailand to the north. Covering 795 square kilometers, this charming state is renowned for its sprawling countryside, featuring paddy fields, rustic villages, limestone outcrops, and tranquil lakes. It is also home to Malaysia's only semi-deciduous forest and a variety of fascinating caves.

Perlis offers a unique blend of natural attractions and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore fruit farms and plantations, where mangoes, grapes, and paddy flourish. In Kangar, the state capital, you can enjoy the historical ambiance of sites like the 97-year-old Syed Alwi Mosque. Nearby, the royal town of Arau adds to the state's rich cultural tapestry. Discover the unhurried pace of life and the distinct charm of this northern gem.

Tanjung Rhu Beach

Located in the northern part of Perlis, Tanjung Rhu Beach is known for its serene environment, clear waters, and picturesque scenery. It’s an ideal spot for a relaxing day by the sea.

Kuala Perlis Beach

This beach near the town of Kuala Perlis is known for its fishing village atmosphere and offers a more laid-back beach experience.

Pantai Sri Tujuh

Slightly removed from town, Pantai Sri Tujuh is a charming beach featuring a unique formation of seven bays. Here, you can watch local fishermen cast their nets and enjoy a variety of water sports, including kayaking, boat rides, and swan boat rides.

The beach is lined with food stalls offering freshly cooked local dishes, perfect for savoring while enjoying the scenic views. Pantai Sri Tujuh is located about seven kilometers from Kota Bharu in Tumpat. Visitors can take bus number 43 from the Kota Bharu Bus Terminal or enjoy a 15-minute walk from the Tumpat train station.

Arked Niaga Padang Besar

Your first stop in Perlis is the bustling Arked Niaga Padang Besar, a vibrant marketplace renowned for its diverse range of stalls and vendors. Located near the Thailand border, this bazaar offers a mix of casual clothing, local snacks, knick-knacks, and unique Perlis souvenirs. It's an ideal spot for both local and international items at budget-friendly prices, all under one roof. Arked Niaga Padang Besar operates from Monday to Saturday, 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM.

To reach Arked Niaga Padang Besar, visitors can take the ETS train from KL Sentral to Padang Besar Train Station, followed by a taxi or e-hailing service to the market. Alternatively, take a bus to Terminal Bukit Lagi in Kangar and then board the MyBus Perlis number T11, which will take you directly to Padang Besar.

Padang Waremart

Opposite Arked Niaga Padang Besar is Padang Waremart, a large shopping complex known for its impressive selection of specialty products and unique snacks. This popular destination stands out for offering a variety of treats and items that you won't find elsewhere. Padang Waremart is open daily from 9:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

To get there, follow similar directions as for Arked Niaga Padang Besar. Take a bus to Terminal Bukit Lagi in Kangar and then board MyBus Perlis number T11 to Padang Besar. Alternatively, take the ETS train from KL Sentral to Padang Besar Train Station and catch a taxi or e-hailing service to the complex.

Pasar Nat Pokok Getah

No shopping trip is complete without a visit to Pasar Nat Pokok Getah, an iconic flea market open from Friday to Sunday, 7:00 AM to 1:00 PM. Nestled in a large rubber tree plantation, the market offers a diverse range of fresh fruits, vegetables, and seafood, as well as a variety of clothing—both new and pre-loved. Shoppers can also find affordable second-hand household items like cookware, radios, and televisions.

To reach Pasar Nat Pokok Getah, take the KTM to Arau Royal Railway Station. From there, it's just an eight-minute drive by taxi or e-hailing service to the market.

Kangar Town

As the largest town in Perlis, Kangar is often the first stop for visitors. Despite its modern developments, Kangar maintains its charming small-town atmosphere, surrounded by lush paddy fields. With a wide range of hotels and restaurants, it serves as an excellent base for exploring the region.

Gua Kelam Recreational Parks

Located in Perlis' limestone hills, Gua Kelam, or 'Cave of Darkness,' is renowned for its captivating cave walks. Situated in the Wang Mu Forest Reserve, which spans over 2,000 hectares, the cave is divided into two sections: Gua Kelam 1 and Gua Kelam 2. Once a site for tin mining, the cave earned its name from the pitch-black darkness experienced by miners who used carbide lamps. Today, the cave is well-lit for visitors to explore safely.

Gua Kelam 1 is particularly popular, featuring an 8-foot wide wooden suspension bridge that offers stunning views of the cave's interior. Designed by an Englishman, the limestone path was originally used for transporting tin from the nearby stream entrance. Visitors can observe live bats hanging from the ceiling, iron trolleys once used for tin transport, and wooden joists that served as walkways for miners. The soothing sounds of the river below add to the cave's tranquil ambiance.

For a more adventurous exploration of Gua Kelam, venture into Gua Kelam 2, which features both a dry and a wet trail. The dry trail leads through a spacious cave where you can see clear blue lakes and historical drawings depicting the lives of past tin miners. For a more thrilling experience, the wet trail takes you through narrower passages where you may need to crawl and swim through a river, navigating tight spaces and testing your balance as you make your way to the exit.

Timah Tasoh Lake

Timah Tasoh Lake, located in Perlis, is a man-made reservoir spanning 1,300 hectares, established in 1987 and completed in 1992. Named after the rivers Sungai Timah and Sungai Tasoh, the lake serves as a vital water catchment area and flood prevention system, holding up to 35.3 million litres of water. Surrounded by lush greenery and rolling hills, the lake is renowned for its serene beauty and is a sanctuary for migrating birds, with over 135 species visiting between October and March.

The lake has been designated a key area for bird conservation by the International Bird Life and is also significant for fish breeding and aquaculture. Its tranquil environment makes it an ideal spot for relaxation, especially during sunset. Visitors can reach Timah Tasoh Lake by taking the North-South Expressway to Arau, Kangar, and Kuala Perlis, or via public transport by taking the ETS to Bukit Keteri and then a short taxi ride, or by bus from Terminal Bus Sepadu to Kangar and then bus number T11 to Beseri.

Wang Kelian Viewpoint

Perlis offers stunning natural landscapes, including lush forests and intriguing caves, perfect for nature enthusiasts. Among its highlights is the Wang Kelian Viewpoint, located in the Hutan Simpanan Mata Ayer within Taman Negeri Perlis. From this vantage point, you can enjoy panoramic views of Perlis' rolling hills and migratory birds soaring past Banjaran Nakawan. The viewpoint is particularly spectacular at sunrise, with its sweeping vistas of cloud-covered landscapes.

To reach Wang Kelian Viewpoint, you can take the ETS from KL Sentral to Padang Besar Railway Station, a short 17-minute drive away. Alternatively, use public transport by taking a bus from Terminal Bersepadu Selatan to Kangar Bus Terminal, then board bus number T11 to Kaki Bukit. From there, it’s just a brief five-minute journey to the viewpoint.

Perlis Herbal Forest

Plant enthusiasts will find Perlis Herbal Forest fascinating. Established in 1998 by the Perlis Forestry Department, this 12-hectare park features a diverse collection of over 1,000 wild and medicinal plants. Highlights include Tongkat Ali, Kacip Fatimah, and Misai Kucing, offering a unique glimpse into the region's herbal flora.

Masjid Al Hussain

Masjid Al Hussain in Kuala Perlis is a striking example of modern Islamic tropical architecture, renowned for its floating design and natural elements. Built atop stilts over the sea, this mosque creates the illusion of floating on water and is adorned with coral stones, broken quartz, marble, granite, and pebble stones. The mosque offers breathtaking views of the Kuala Perlis shoreline, especially at sunset when the golden-orange hues enhance its beauty.

A prominent religious and cultural landmark, Masjid Al Hussain accommodates over 1,000 worshippers in its main and multipurpose prayer halls. Designed by His Highness Raja Muda of Perlis Tuanku Syed Faizuddin Putra, the mosque features a unique hexagonal twin peak minaret, symbolizing the six tenets of Iman in Islam. Its illuminated minaret, which shines with colorful lights during the azan, serves as a beacon for prayer times for those at sea. This mosque not only fulfills a religious purpose but also stands as a testament to architectural innovation and cultural heritage.

Istana Jahar

Istana Jahar, located in Kota Bharu, has been a local museum since 1992, dedicated to showcasing the regal customs of Kelantan. Originally known as Istana Raja Bendahara Long Kundur bin Sultan Ahmad, the palace was converted into a museum and named after the Jahar tree that grows on the grounds.

This two-story museum displays a wealth of royal artifacts, including textiles, jewelry, royal attire, copperware, and a collection of Malay weapons. The building itself reflects the essence of Kelantanese royalty through its traditional design and architecture.

Kelantan State Museum

The Kelantan State Museum, officially opened in 1990, occupies the former Kota Bharu Land Office and Kota Bharu Council Office. It houses a diverse collection of local artifacts that illustrate the history of Kelantan from its early days. Among the exhibits is a cave replica, along with displays of Malaysia's renowned Wayang Kulit (shadow puppets), Mak Yong (traditional dance-drama), and various traditional musical instruments and games. The museum also features temporary exhibits in its foyer.

Located in the heart of Kota Bharu, the museum is easily accessible by car, taxi, or a short walk. Its central location makes it convenient for visitors to explore other nearby attractions.

Kangar Street Art

Kangar Street Art is a 100-meter walkway along Jalan Lencongan 2, adorned with 80 vibrant murals celebrating the heritage, small-town charm, and creativity of Perlis. Under the theme 'Colours of Perlis,' these lively artworks were created by 30 student artists. Be sure to spot murals featuring the Awang Batil, Kangar Book Store, and more!
